Token Overview

The FLOW token serves as the cornerstone of the HyperFlow Protocol ecosystem, providing governance rights, revenue sharing, and access to advanced DeFi infrastructure. With a fixed supply of 1 billion tokens and community-focused distribution, FLOW establishes sustainable value creation for all stakeholders.

Key Token Benefits

  • Revenue Sharing: 50% of all protocol fees distributed to token holders
  • Governance Power: Community voting on protocol parameters and upgrades
  • Fee Discounts: Reduced costs across all platform services
  • Yield Enhancement: Boosted APY for FLOW holders in select vaults

Automated Distribution

Revenue sharing operates through smart contracts with automated distribution every 24 hours. Token holders earn rewards proportional to their staked FLOW tokens, creating sustainable passive income streams.

Token Utility Mechanisms

Governance Voting

FLOW holders participate in protocol governance with 1 token = 1 vote. Decisions include fee adjustments, new strategy approvals, and platform upgrades.

Staking Rewards

Stake FLOW tokens to earn 50% of protocol revenue. Rewards automatically compound and can be claimed anytime without penalties.

Fee Discounts

FLOW holders receive reduced fees across all platform services: 25% discount for 1K+ tokens, 50% discount for 10K+ tokens.

Yield Boosting

Enhanced APY in select vaults for FLOW holders. Boost multiplier ranges from 1.1x to 2.0x based on token holdings and lock duration.

Priority Access

Early access to new strategies, beta features, and exclusive investment opportunities. Premium support and advanced analytics included.

Liquidity Mining

Provide FLOW liquidity on DEXs to earn additional rewards. LP rewards complement protocol revenue sharing for maximized returns.

Economic Incentives

Value Accrual Mechanisms

Supply Management

Deflationary Pressure

HyperFlow implements multiple deflationary mechanisms including revenue buybacks, governance fee burns, and optional staking lock periods. These reduce circulating supply while increasing utility demand.
